# Laboratory Teaching Assistant – Basic Electronics & Analog/Digital Electronics

This repository contains the official certificate and supporting material documenting my work as a Laboratory Teaching Assistant in the Department of Electrical and Electronic Engineering.

During this role, I supported undergraduate laboratory courses in:

- Basic Electronics
- Analog Electronics & Digital Electronics

My responsibilities included assisting students during laboratory exercises, explaining theoretical concepts, troubleshooting circuits, and grading laboratory assignments.

## Responsibilities

As a Laboratory Teaching Assistant, I was responsible for:

- Assisting students in understanding laboratory exercises
- Explaining electronic circuit theory and measurement procedures
- Helping students build and test circuits on breadboards
- Troubleshooting wiring and measurement errors
- Supporting the use of laboratory instruments
- Evaluating and grading laboratory assignments and reports
- Providing feedback on both theoretical and practical work

- Oscilloscopes
- Function generators

- Digital multimeters

## Topics Covered

### Basic Electronics

- Ohm’s Law
- Kirchhoff’s Voltage and Current Laws
- Series and parallel resistor networks
- Voltage dividers
- Capacitor charging and discharging
- RC and RL circuits
- Diodes and rectifier circuits

### Analog Electronics

- Bipolar Junction Transistors (BJTs)
- MOSFET fundamentals
- Biasing circuits
- Common-emitter amplifiers
- Operational amplifiers
- Filters
- Gain and frequency response analysis

### Digital Electronics

- Logic gates
- Truth tables
- Boolean algebra
- Karnaugh maps
- Combinational circuits
- Sequential circuits
- Flip-flops
- Counters and registers
